Pi Du Mian (Nanjing-style Pork Skin Noodles):

Description:

Pi Du Mian, or Nanjing-Style Pork Skin Noodles, is a savory and slightly spicy noodle dish crafted with ingredients such as pork skin, noodles, bok choy, and an array of delightful garnishes. It holds a cherished place in the hearts of Nanjing locals, representing a beloved local delicacy. The recipe for this dish varies widely across households and street food vendors, each offering its unique twist. Here, the version I'm sharing is the one Iâ€™m most familiar with growing up.

Ingredients:

  • 200g noodles
  • 100g pork liver
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 100g bok choy
  • 20g salted spicy radish
  • 50g shredded pork
  • 50g wood ear
  • 40g dried pork skin
  • 400g chicken broth
  • A pinch of salt
  • 30g Chinese cooking wine
  • A pinch of potato starch
  • Chinese spicy chili oil

Instructions:

  1. Soak dried pork skin and wood ear in water till softens and cut into pieces
  2. Marinate the shredded pork and pork liver with cooking wine, salt and starch
  3. Cut tomatoes into slices and bok choy into pieces
  4. Stir fry the pork liver until it changes color
  5. Prepare another pot of boiling water and cook the noodles
  6. Put broth in the stir-fry pot
  7. Put in everything besides shredded pork and liver
  8. Put in shredded pork until it's cooked
  9. Put in pork liver and wait till boil
  10. Add a pinch of salt and chili oil for seasoning

Sources and References:

This particular culinary tradition holds a special place in my hometown, which is why most of the available references are in Chinese. The recipe is a culmination of information gathered from various sources, as different families have their unique ways of preparing these noodles. I have taken on the task of translating these Chinese sources into the recipe and description, though it's important to note that some nuances may be lost in translation.
